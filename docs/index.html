claude · meter · v0.2

Two rings on the corner of your screen.

A pinned overlay that keeps your Claude Code 5-hour and weekly budgets visible without taking up real estate. The fill is your % spent. The white spoke marks where a fair pace would put you right now. Color answers am I burning faster than my budget? at a glance. Drag the sliders below — the rings move the same way they do live.

Reading the ring

The bright fill shows your % used. The thin white spoke marks where a fair pace would put you right now — derived from Anthropic's own resets_at timestamp, not a guess from your transcript timestamps. Fill past the spoke means you're burning faster than evenly; behind it means headroom.

What the color says

Color is the gap between fill and spoke. Cyan when you're well behind the spoke, lime when on pace, soft periwinkle as you start to creep ahead, deep violet when you're well past it. No alarms — just at-a-glance answers.

Click the chevron, get a dot

The top-right corner has a small >. Click it and the meter shrinks to a 22px urgency-colored dot in the same spot — out of the way of Spotlight and menu dropdowns, but still pulsing in your 5h color. Click the dot to expand back.

Refresh button — explicit, cheap

The circular arrow next to the chevron. Click it to spend ~160 tokens (a fraction of a cent, rounding error against your 5h budget) on one tiny claude --print call, which forces a fresh rate-limit reading. Disabled while in flight so you can't double-spend.

Source of truth

claude-meter reads the same numbers Claude Code's own /usage panel displays, via a statusline hook that captures rate_limits.five_hour and rate_limits.seven_day straight from Anthropic. No estimates, no guessed ceilings.

Stale pill — honest about freshness

If the rate-limits file hasn't been refreshed in 90+ seconds, a small orange stale Nm pill appears under the verdict. The meter never silently lies about how current the data is.

Install

# clone, install, run — that's it
git clone https://github.com/CasterlyGit/claude-meter
cd claude-meter
python3.12 -m venv .venv && source .venv/bin/activate
pip install -e .
claude-meter # overlay appears top-right of your rightmost display
